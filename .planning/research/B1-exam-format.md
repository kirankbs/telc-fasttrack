## 1. Section-by-Section Specification

### Overview

| Section | Duration | Parts | Items | Points | Weight |
|---------|----------|-------|-------|--------|--------|
| Leseverstehen (Reading) | 90 min (shared with Sprachbausteine) | 3 | 20 | 75 | 25% |
| Sprachbausteine (Language Elements) | (included in 90 min) | 2 | 20 | 30 | 10% |
| Hoerverstehen (Listening) | ~30 min | 3 | 20 | 75 | 25% |
| Schriftlicher Ausdruck (Writing) | 30 min | 1 | 1 | 45 | 15% |
| Muendlicher Ausdruck (Speaking) | 15 min (+20 prep) | 3 | 3 | 75 | 25% |
| **Total** | **~150 min written + 15 min oral** | | | **300** | |

Written block = 225 pts (Lesen 75 + Sprachbausteine 30 + Hoeren 75 + Schreiben 45).
Oral block = 75 pts.
Pass: 60% in EACH block independently: 135/225 written AND 45/75 oral.

### Leseverstehen (Reading) -- 75 pts, 3 parts

**Teil 1 -- Globalverstehen (5 items, 25 pts)**
- Task: Match 5 headings (from a pool of 10 headings a-j) to 5 short texts.
- Text type: Short informational passages (80-120 words each) -- typically newspaper snippets, magazine blurbs, or notices about events, services, courses.
- Question type: Matching (Zuordnung). Each heading used once; 5 are distractors.
- Points: 5 pts per correct match.

**Teil 2 -- Detailverstehen (5 items, 25 pts)**
- Task: Read one longer text (~350-450 words), answer 5 MCQ (a/b/c).
- Text type: Semi-formal or journalistic text -- a newspaper article, magazine feature, or informational report. Topics: everyday life, work, health, education, leisure.
- Question type: MCQ with 3 options. Questions follow text sequence.
- Points: 5 pts per correct answer.

**Teil 3 -- Selektives Verstehen (10 items, 25 pts)**
- Task: Match 10 situations/needs to 12 short ads/notices (a-l). Mark "x" if no ad fits.
- Text type: Classified ads, event listings, course offerings, job ads, notices from community boards.
- Question type: Matching. Each ad used at most once. 2 ads are distractors.
- Points: 2.5 pts per correct match.

### Sprachbausteine -- 30 pts, 2 parts

**Teil 1 -- Grammar Cloze (10 items, 15 pts)**
- Format: One continuous text (~150-200 words, typically a personal or semi-formal letter/email) with 10 numbered gaps.
- Question type: MCQ with 3 options per gap (a/b/c).
- Tested: Grammatical accuracy -- prepositions, articles + case, verb forms (Perfekt/Praeteritum/modals), conjunctions + word order, pronouns, adjective endings, negation.
- Points: 1.5 pts per correct answer.

**Teil 2 -- Lexical Cloze (10 items, 15 pts)**
- Format: One continuous text (~150-200 words, typically an email or letter) with 10 numbered gaps.
- Question type: Word bank -- choose from 15 options (a-o), 5 are distractors.
- Tested: Vocabulary in context -- verb+preposition combos, fixed phrases, connectors (deshalb, trotzdem, ausserdem), formal/informal register words, standard letter phrases.
- Points: 1.5 pts per correct answer.

### Hoerverstehen (Listening) -- 75 pts, 3 parts

**Teil 1 -- 5 items, 25 pts**
- Task: Listen to 5 short dialogues/monologues. For each, decide: richtig or falsch.
- Audio: Everyday conversations (at the doctor, on the phone, at work). Each ~30-60 seconds.
- Playback: Heard ONCE only.
- Points: 5 pts per correct answer.

**Teil 2 -- 10 items, 25 pts**
- Task: Listen to a longer broadcast/interview (~3-4 minutes). Answer 10 statements: richtig or falsch.
- Audio: Radio report, interview, or informational broadcast on topics like health, travel, education.
- Playback: Heard TWICE.
- Points: 2.5 pts per correct answer.

**Teil 3 -- 5 items, 25 pts**
- Task: Listen to a conversation between two people (~3 minutes). Answer 5 MCQ (a/b/c).
- Audio: Extended dialogue -- friends planning, colleagues discussing, customer service call.
- Playback: Heard TWICE.
- Points: 5 pts per correct answer.

### Schriftlicher Ausdruck (Writing) -- 45 pts, 1 task

- Task: Write a semi-formal letter/email responding to a given prompt.
- Word count: ~150 words (guidance range ~120-180 words).
- Prompt format: You receive a short situation description and 3-4 Leitpunkte (guiding points) you must address.
- Text types: Thank-you letter, complaint, inquiry, invitation response, arrangement change.

**Scoring criteria (3 dimensions):**

| Criterion | Description | Impact |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| I. Inhaltliche Vollstaendigkeit | All Leitpunkte addressed? | If rated "D" (not met) -> entire letter scores 0 |
| II. Kommunikative Gestaltung | Coherent structure, greeting/closing, register, connectors | Bonus point possible for exceptional quality |
| III. Formale Richtigkeit | Grammar, spelling, punctuation | If rated "D" -> entire letter scores 0 |

This is critical for mock design: a letter that addresses all Leitpunkte but has poor grammar can still pass, while a grammatically perfect letter that misses Leitpunkte scores zero.

### Muendlicher Ausdruck (Speaking) -- 75 pts, 3 parts

Preparation: 20 minutes before exam. Candidates receive task sheets for all 3 parts.
Format: Pair exam (Paarpruefung) with 2 candidates. Two licensed telc examiners present.

**Teil 1 -- Kontaktaufnahme (~3 min, 25 pts)**
- Task: Introduce yourself. Ask your partner questions to get to know them.
- Topics: Name, origin, family, work/study, hobbies, living situation.
- Interaction required -- not just a monologue.

**Teil 2 -- Gespraech ueber ein Thema (~6 min, 25 pts)**
- Task: Each candidate receives different information (photo, graphic, or short text) on the same topic. Present your material, then discuss with partner.
- Topics: Media use, health, education, travel, work-life balance.
- Must share experiences and opinions.

**Teil 3 -- Gemeinsam eine Aufgabe loesen (~6 min, 25 pts)**
- Task: Plan something together with your partner -- reach agreement.
- Scenarios: Plan a party, organize a trip, choose a gift, arrange an event.
- Must make proposals, respond to partner's suggestions, negotiate, reach a decision.

**Scoring dimensions (applied across all 3 parts):**

| Dimension | What examiners assess |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Aufgabenbewaeltigung | Task completion, interaction quality, initiative |
| Ausdruecksfaehigkeit | Range of vocabulary and structures, fluency |
| Formale Richtigkeit | Grammatical accuracy, self-correction |
| Aussprache/Intonation | Intelligibility, natural rhythm |

## 2. Sprachbausteine Deep-Dive

This section is NEW at B1 -- not present at A1/A2. It is the single most "drill-able" section, making it a high-value target for app content.

### Grammar Points Tested in Teil 1 (ranked by frequency)

1. **Prepositions + case** -- mit + Dat, fuer + Akk, seit + Dat, wegen + Gen. The most common gap type.
2. **Verb forms** -- Perfekt (haben/sein selection, Partizip II), Praeteritum of modals (konnte, musste, durfte), Konjunktiv II (wuerde, koennte, haette).
3. **Conjunctions + word order** -- weil/dass/wenn push verb to end; deshalb/trotzdem keep verb in position 2. This is THE trap -- candidates confuse subordinating and coordinating conjunctions.
4. **Articles + case endings** -- Dative after prepositions (in der Stadt, mit dem Bus), accusative for direction.
5. **Pronouns** -- Reflexive (sich freuen), personal in Dat/Akk (mir/mich, ihm/ihn), possessive (mein/meine/meinem).
6. **Adjective endings** -- After definite/indefinite articles in different cases. Notoriously difficult.
7. **Infinitiv + zu** -- vergessen zu, anfangen zu, um...zu constructions.
8. **Relative pronouns** -- der/die/das matching gender and case of antecedent.
9. **Negation** -- kein vs. nicht placement.
10. **Comparative/superlative** -- so...wie vs. -er...als.

### Distractor Patterns in Teil 1

- **Case trap**: All 3 options are the same word but different case (dem/den/der). Forces grammatical reasoning.
- **Conjunction swap**: weil vs. deshalb vs. trotzdem. Tests whether candidate knows which triggers verb-final.
- **Tense confusion**: Present vs. Perfekt vs. Praeteritum forms of the same verb.
- **Preposition near-miss**: an/auf/in -- all plausible with the noun, but only one is idiomatic (am Montag, not auf Montag).

### Teil 2 Strategy Notes

- Texts are almost always letters/emails -- knowing standard letter phrases gives 3-4 "free" answers:
  - "Vielen Dank fuer Ihre/deine Nachricht"
  - "Ich wuerde mich freuen, wenn..."
  - "Mit freundlichen Gruessen" / "Liebe Gruesse"
- Connectors (zuerst, dann, danach, ausserdem, deshalb, trotzdem) fill ~30% of gaps.
- Verb+preposition combos are the hardest: warten AUF, sich freuen UEBER, teilnehmen AN -- these must be memorized as chunks.

## 4. Grammar Scope for B1

### New at B1 (beyond A2 mastery)

| Topic | How it appears in telc B1 | Testing method |
|-------|---------------------------|----------------|
| Konjunktiv II | wuerde + Infinitiv, haette, waere, koennte | Sprachbausteine gap-fill, polite requests in Schreiben |
| Passiv (Praesens) | Das Haus wird gebaut | Lesen comprehension, Sprachbausteine |
| Relativsaetze | Der Mann, der dort wohnt, ... | Sprachbausteine (relative pronoun selection) |
| Plusquamperfekt | Nachdem ich gegessen hatte, ... | Lesen comprehension (narrative texts) |
| Infinitiv + zu | Ich habe vergessen, anzurufen. | Sprachbausteine Teil 1 |
| Indirekte Rede (basic) | Er sagt, dass er kommen wuerde. | Lesen, occasionally Sprachbausteine |
| Praepos. + Genitiv | wegen, trotz, waehrend, ausserhalb | Sprachbausteine, Lesen |
| Advanced connectors | deshalb, trotzdem, ausserdem, obwohl, damit, um...zu | Sprachbausteine (both parts), Schreiben |
| n-Deklination | den Herrn, dem Kollegen | Rare but appears in Sprachbausteine |

### A2 Grammar That Must Be Solid

These are NOT explicitly tested as "new" topics but underpin every B1 task:
- Perfekt with haben/sein (which auxiliary?)
- Dative case (after mit, zu, bei, von, aus, nach, seit, gegenueber)
- Two-way prepositions (an, auf, in, ueber, unter, vor, hinter, neben, zwischen)
- Subordinate clauses with weil, dass, wenn (verb-final)
- Comparative and superlative
- Reflexive verbs

## 6. Schreiben Task Specification

### Prompt Format (for mock authoring)

A B1 Schreiben task consists of:

1. **Situation** (2-3 sentences): Sets the context. Example: "Ihr Freund Marco hat Sie zu seiner Geburtstagsfeier eingeladen. Sie koennen leider nicht kommen."
2. **3-4 Leitpunkte**: Specific content the candidate must address. Example:
   - Warum koennen Sie nicht kommen?
   - Schlagen Sie einen anderen Termin vor.
   - Was moechten Sie Marco schenken?
3. **Format instruction**: "Schreiben Sie einen Brief / eine E-Mail (~150 Woerter)."

### What Makes a Good Schreiben Prompt

- Situation is realistic and relatable (not abstract or niche)
- Leitpunkte require different communicative functions: explaining, suggesting, requesting, thanking
- Register is clear from context (Du vs. Sie -- friend vs. landlord)
- All 3-4 Leitpunkte are independently addressable (no Leitpunkt depends on another)
- Word count target of ~150 allows addressing all points without padding

### Scoring Quick Reference

45 pts total. Three criteria scored on A-D scale, then converted:
- A (very good) = 15 pts
- B (good) = 10 pts
- C (adequate) = 5 pts
- D (inadequate) = 0 pts AND triggers automatic 0 for entire task if applied to criteria I or III

## 7. Sprechen Tasks

### Teil 1: Kontaktaufnahme (~3 min)

Candidates introduce themselves and ask the partner questions. Not scripted -- must show spontaneous interaction. Key phrases needed:
- "Ich komme aus... / Ich lebe in..."
- "Was machen Sie beruflich?" / "Arbeitest du?"
- "Was sind Ihre Hobbys?"
- Follow-up questions: "Ach, das ist interessant. Seit wann machen Sie das?"

### Teil 2: Gespraech ueber ein Thema (~6 min)

Each candidate gets different material (image, graph, short text) on the same topic. Sequence:
1. Describe your material to your partner
2. Partner describes theirs
3. Discuss: share opinions, experiences, ask questions

Sample topics: "Kinder und Medien", "Gesund leben", "Reisen mit dem Zug vs. Fliegen"

### Teil 3: Gemeinsam planen (~6 min)

A shared planning task. Candidates receive a task card with bullet points to discuss:
- Was? Wann? Wo? Wer? Was brauchen wir? Was kostet es?

Sample: "Sie moechten zusammen ein Abschiedsfest fuer eine Kollegin organisieren."

### Preparation Time

20 minutes before the oral exam. Candidates receive all 3 task cards and can take notes. This prep time is a key differentiator from A1/A2 (which have no prep time).

## 8. Quality Markers for Strong B1 Mocks

### What makes a question STRONG

1. **Answer requires reading/listening, not general knowledge.** A question like "Was ist die Hauptstadt von Deutschland?" tests world knowledge, not reading comprehension. A strong question has the answer embedded in the text, and the candidate must locate and interpret it.

2. **Distractors are plausible within the text.** In MCQ, wrong answers should use words that appear in the text but answer a different question. Example: Text mentions both "Montag" and "Mittwoch" -- if the question asks when the event starts, one wrong answer should use the other day mentioned.

3. **Questions test B1-level processing, not A2.** B1 reading means understanding main ideas, opinions, and implicit information -- not just finding a phone number. A strong B1 question asks "Why does the author recommend..." not "What time does the shop open?"

4. **Sprachbausteine gaps test genuine grammar decisions, not vocabulary recall.** A gap that can only be filled by knowing one specific word is vocabulary testing (B1 Sprachbausteine Teil 2). A gap where the candidate must choose between "dem" and "den" based on case logic is grammar testing (Teil 1). Keep these distinct.

5. **Listening audio uses natural speech patterns.** Speakers use filler words (also, na ja, eigentlich), contractions, and natural hesitations. Pure Hochdeutsch read-aloud is unrealistic and under-prepares candidates.

### What makes a question WEAK

1. **Text is artificially constructed around the questions.** If a text reads like it was written to contain specific answer words, it lacks authentic register. Real B1 texts have information density -- not every sentence maps to a question.

2. **All MCQ options are obviously wrong except one.** If options b and c are absurd, the question tests nothing. At B1, all three options should be defensible at first glance.

3. **Sprachbausteine text is grammatically unnatural even with correct answers filled in.** The completed text should read as a natural letter. If filling all gaps correctly produces stilted prose, the item is poorly constructed.

4. **Listening scripts use vocabulary above B1.** Words like "Infrastruktur", "Marginalisierung", or "Rezession" do not belong in B1 audio. The Goethe B1 Wortliste is the ceiling.

5. **Writing prompts are too open-ended or too constraining.** "Write about your life" is too open (no Leitpunkte to score against). "Write exactly what happened on Tuesday at 3pm when you met Herr Mueller at the post office" is too constraining (becomes gap-fill with sentences).

## 9. Pitfalls When Authoring B1 Mocks

### Common mistakes by new authors

1. **A2 vocabulary leaking into "B1" texts.** A text using only words like Haus, Schule, Supermarkt, essen, trinken reads as A2 regardless of topic. B1 texts should use Meinung, Erfahrung, Vorteil, vorschlagen, vereinbaren -- words that signal opinion-forming and planning.

2. **Questions answerable without the text.** "Ist Sport gesund?" -- anyone can answer this. The question must require the specific text. Better: "Was empfiehlt der Autor fuer Menschen, die wenig Zeit haben?"

3. **Uniform sentence length and structure.** Real German texts mix short declarative sentences with longer subordinate clause constructions. A text where every sentence is 8-12 words with SVO order is artificially simple.

4. **Ignoring the text-type conventions.** A B1 "Zeitungsartikel" should have a headline, a lead sentence, and body paragraphs. A "Brief" needs Anrede, body, and Grussformel. Mixing these conventions breaks authenticity.

5. **Sprachbausteine where multiple options are grammatically correct.** Every gap must have exactly one correct answer. If "dem" and "einem" both work, the item is broken. Pre-test by having someone else attempt the gap without seeing the key.

6. **Listening scripts that are too slow or too clear.** B1 audio should be at natural-to-slightly-slow pace (0.95x per our TTS spec). Exaggerated pauses between words or hyper-articulated consonants create false confidence.

7. **Writing prompts with unclear register.** If the situation says "your friend" but uses Sie-pronouns in the prompt text, candidates won't know whether to write formally or informally. Register must be unambiguous.

8. **Recycling the same topic across parts.** If Lesen Teil 2 is about "Gesundheit" and Hoeren Teil 2 is also about "Gesundheit" in the same mock, the candidate gets redundant exposure. Spread topics: health, work, education, travel, media, environment across parts.

9. **Teil 3 Lesen ads that are too short to discriminate.** Each ad needs enough detail that candidates must read carefully to distinguish it from similar options. "Deutschkurs, Montag 18 Uhr" is too sparse. Include location, price, prerequisites, contact -- the matching requires scanning multiple details.

10. **Forgetting the "x" option in Teil 3 Lesen.** Some situations should genuinely have no matching ad. If every situation has a match, the "x" option becomes decoration and candidates learn to ignore it.
