## 1. Section-by-Section Specification

### Overview

| Section | Duration | Parts | Items | Points | Weight |
|---------|----------|-------|-------|--------|--------|
| Leseverstehen (Reading) | 90 min (shared with Sprachbausteine) | 3 | 20 | 75 | 25% |
| Sprachbausteine (Language Elements) | (included in 90 min) | 2 | 20 | 30 | 10% |
| Hoerverstehen (Listening) | ~20 min | 3 | 20 | 75 | 25% |
| Schriftlicher Ausdruck (Writing) | 30 min | 1 | 1 (choose 1 of 2) | 45 | 15% |
| Muendlicher Ausdruck (Speaking) | ~15 min (+20 prep) | 3 | 3 | 75 | 25% |
| **Total** | **~140 min written + 15 min oral** | | | **300** | |

Written block = 225 pts (Lesen 75 + Sprachbausteine 30 + Hoeren 75 + Schreiben 45).
Oral block = 75 pts.
Pass: 60% in EACH block independently: **135/225 written AND 45/75 oral**.

Unlike A1 and A2, B2 **CAN be taken as a partial exam** -- if you fail one block (written or oral), you may retake only that block within 1 calendar year.

### Leseverstehen (Reading) -- 75 pts, 3 parts

**Teil 1 -- Globalverstehen (5 items, 25 pts)**
- Task: Match 5 headings (from a pool of 10 headings a-j) to 5 short texts.
- Text type: Short informational passages -- newspaper articles, magazine snippets, informational blurbs. Texts are more abstract than B1: media commentary, workplace trends, educational policy, cultural observations.
- Question type: Matching (Zuordnung). Each heading used once; 5 are distractors.
- Points: 5 pts per correct match.
- B2 difference from B1: Texts contain more implicit information. Headings may paraphrase rather than quote directly. Candidates must infer the main idea, not just scan for keywords.

**Teil 2 -- Detailverstehen (5 items, 25 pts)**
- Task: Read one longer text (~450-600 words), answer 5 MCQ (a/b/c).
- Text type: Journalistic article, opinion piece, or feature report. Topics: professional development, social trends, technology impact, environmental policy, cultural phenomena.
- Question type: MCQ with 3 options. Questions follow text sequence but require inference, not just surface reading.
- Points: 5 pts per correct answer.
- B2 difference from B1: Text length increases by ~100-150 words. Questions test implicature and author intent, not just factual recall. Distractors use vocabulary from the text but apply it to a different claim.

**Teil 3 -- Selektives Verstehen (10 items, 25 pts)**
- Task: Match 10 situations/needs to 12 informative texts (a-l). Mark "x" if no text fits.
- Text type: Short informational texts -- job postings, seminar descriptions, course listings, conference announcements, professional service ads.
- Question type: Matching. Each text used at most once. 2 texts are distractors.
- Points: 2.5 pts per correct match.
- B2 difference from B1: Situations are more nuanced (not just "someone looking for a cooking class" but "a manager seeking a conflict resolution workshop for her team"). Texts use professional/semi-formal register.

### Sprachbausteine -- 30 pts, 2 parts

**Teil 1 -- Grammar/Lexical Cloze (10 items, 15 pts)**
- Format: One continuous text (~200-250 words) with 10 numbered gaps. Typically a semi-formal or formal text -- a newspaper column, a letter to a colleague, or an informational report.
- Question type: MCQ with **4 options per gap (a/b/c/d)** -- this is a key difference from B1, which uses only 3 options.
- Tested: Advanced grammar and vocabulary in context -- Konjunktiv II in polite/hypothetical contexts, Passiv and Passiv-Ersatzformen, Partizipialattribute, advanced preposition+case combos, Nominalisierung, complex connectors (je...desto, sowohl...als auch, nicht nur...sondern auch), fixed verb+preposition phrases at B2 level.
- Points: 1.5 pts per correct answer.

**Teil 2 -- Lexical Cloze (10 items, 15 pts)**
- Format: One continuous text (~200-250 words) with 10 numbered gaps.
- Question type: Word bank -- choose from **15 options (a-o)**, 5 are distractors.
- Tested: Vocabulary precision in context -- connectors and discourse markers (dennoch, hingegen, demzufolge, insofern), Funktionsverbgefuege (in Betracht ziehen, zur Verfuegung stellen), nominalized verbs and adjectives, register-appropriate synonyms, idiomatic fixed phrases.
- Points: 1.5 pts per correct answer.

### How B2 Sprachbausteine Differ from B1

| Dimension | B1 | B2 |
|-----------|----|----|
| Teil 1 options per gap | 3 (a/b/c) | 4 (a/b/c/d) |
| Teil 2 word bank size | 15 (a-o) | 15 (a-o) -- same pool size |
| Text register | Personal letter/email | Semi-formal to formal text |
| Grammar focus | Basic Konjunktiv II, Perfekt/Praeteritum, prepositions+case | Passiv with modals, Konjunktiv I, Partizipialattribute, Nominalisierung |
| Vocabulary focus | Everyday connectors (deshalb, trotzdem) | Academic/formal connectors (dennoch, hingegen, insofern) |
| Distractor sophistication | Wrong case or tense | Semantically plausible but contextually wrong; tests collocational knowledge |
| Text complexity | ~150-200 words, simple clause structure | ~200-250 words, embedded clauses, nominal style |

### Hoerverstehen (Listening) -- 75 pts, 3 parts

Critical B2 difference: **ALL audio is played ONCE only.** B1 plays Teil 2 and Teil 3 twice. At B2, nothing is repeated. This is the single biggest difficulty jump for candidates moving from B1 to B2.

**Teil 1 -- Globalverstehen (5 items, 25 pts)**
- Task: Listen to 5 short, unrelated news items/announcements. For each, decide: richtig or falsch based on a given statement.
- Audio: Short news bulletin excerpts, announcements, public information. Each ~30-45 seconds. Formal register, broadcast style.
- Playback: Heard ONCE.
- Points: 5 pts per correct answer.
- Trap: Statements are paraphrased, not verbatim from audio. A statement can be factually true in general but "falsch" because the audio says something different.

**Teil 2 -- Detailverstehen (10 items, 25 pts)**
- Task: Listen to a longer radio interview or feature (~4-5 minutes). Answer 10 statements: richtig or falsch.
- Audio: Extended interview with a guest expert on a B2-level topic (workplace culture, digital education, sustainability, health policy). Two speakers with natural turn-taking.
- Playback: Heard ONCE.
- Points: 2.5 pts per correct answer.
- Trap: The interview contains hedging and qualification ("Ja, das stimmt, aber nur teilweise..."). Candidates must distinguish between what the speaker actually endorses vs. what they mention as someone else's view.

**Teil 3 -- Selektives Verstehen (5 items, 25 pts)**
- Task: Listen to 5 short announcements/presentations. Match each to a statement or complete a matching task.
- Audio: Short talks, announcements, or voicemail-style messages (~30-60 seconds each). Topics: professional situations, event information, service descriptions.
- Playback: Heard ONCE.
- Points: 5 pts per correct answer.

### Schriftlicher Ausdruck (Writing) -- 45 pts, 1 task

**Task format: Choose 1 of 2 prompts.**

This is different from B1, where there is only one prompt. At B2, candidates select the prompt they are more comfortable with.

**Typical prompt types:**
1. Beschwerdebrief (complaint letter) -- write to a company, hotel, service provider about a problem
2. Bitte um Information (request for information) -- write to an institution asking for details about a course, program, event
3. Stellungnahme (opinion letter) -- respond to a newspaper article or statement with a reasoned opinion

**Word count:** ~150-200 words. Guidance often states "mindestens 150 Woerter."

**Prompt structure:**
- Situation description (3-4 sentences)
- 3 Leitpunkte (guiding points) to address, plus sometimes a 4th "eigener Aspekt" (your own aspect)
- Clear register: formal (Sie-form) for complaints/requests, semiformal for opinion pieces

**Scoring criteria (3 dimensions, each A-D, converted to points):**

| Criterion | What is scored | Impact |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| I. Beruecksichtigung der Leitpunkte | All 3 Leitpunkte addressed with sufficient depth? At B2, "sufficient depth" means more than a single complex sentence per point. | Rated D = entire task scores 0 |
| II. Kommunikative Gestaltung | Text cohesion, register consistency, discourse markers, text-type conventions (Anrede, Grussformel for letters), paragraph structure | Contributes to overall impression |
| III. Formale Richtigkeit | Grammar, spelling, punctuation. At B2, errors must not impair understanding. Text should be clearly understandable on a single reading. | Rated D = entire task scores 0 |

**Point conversion:**
- Each criterion scored A (very good) / B (good) / C (adequate) / D (inadequate)
- Conversion: A = 15, B = 10, C = 5, D = 0
- Maximum: 45 pts (3 x 15)
- If criterion I or III is rated D, the ENTIRE writing task scores 0

**B2 difference from B1 writing:**
- Choice of prompts (2 options vs. 1 at B1)
- Higher word count expectation (~150-200 vs. ~120-180)
- Leitpunkte require deeper treatment -- not just mentioning a point but developing it with reasoning/examples
- Register must be consistently formal -- an informal slip in a Beschwerdebrief is penalized
- More demanding text types (complaint, opinion) vs. B1's semi-formal arrangements/invitations

### Muendlicher Ausdruck (Speaking) -- 75 pts, 3 parts

Preparation: 20 minutes before exam. Candidates receive task sheets for all 3 parts.
Format: Pair exam (Paarpruefung) with 2 candidates. Two licensed telc examiners present.

**Teil 1 -- Ueber Erfahrungen sprechen (~5 min total, 25 pts)**
- Task: Choose 1 topic from 7 fixed options and give a ~1.5-2 minute presentation, then answer partner's questions.
- Fixed topic options: a book, a film, a trip, a music event, a sports event, an important person in your life, an important experience.
- Scoring: Fluency, structure (introduction-body-conclusion), vocabulary range, ability to answer follow-up questions spontaneously.
- B2 difference from B1 Teil 1: At B1, Teil 1 is a simple self-introduction. At B2, it is a mini-presentation requiring narrative structure and reflection.

**Teil 2 -- Diskussion (~5 min, 25 pts)**
- Task: Discuss a controversial topic based on a provided text (short article or opinion piece, ~100-150 words).
- Candidates must: state their position, provide arguments and examples, respond to partner's arguments, attempt to find common ground or agree to disagree.
- Topics: digitalization in schools, work-life balance, environmental policy, social media effects, public vs. private healthcare.
- Scoring: Quality of argumentation, ability to react to partner's points, discourse management (interrupting politely, yielding the floor, building on partner's ideas).
- B2 difference from B1 Teil 2: At B1, candidates describe material and share opinions. At B2, they must argue -- defend a position, counter-argue, and engage dialectically.

**Teil 3 -- Gemeinsam etwas planen (~5 min, 25 pts)**
- Task: Plan something together with partner -- reach agreement.
- Scenarios: Organize a farewell party for a colleague, plan a team-building weekend, arrange a charity event, design a language exchange program.
- Must: Make proposals, weigh options, negotiate, address practical details (wann, wo, wer, was kostet es, was brauchen wir), reach a decision.
- B2 difference from B1 Teil 3: Planning scenarios are more professional/complex. Candidates must consider constraints and tradeoffs, not just list preferences.

**Scoring dimensions (applied across all 3 parts):**

| Dimension | What examiners assess |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Ausdruecksfaehigkeit | Vocabulary range and precision, ability to paraphrase, register appropriateness |
| Aufgabenbewaeltigung | Task completion, initiative, interaction quality, discourse management |
| Formale Richtigkeit | Complex structures used correctly (Passiv, Konjunktiv II, subordinate clauses), self-correction |
| Aussprache/Intonation | Intelligibility, sentence stress, natural rhythm, word stress patterns |

**Speaking scoring conversion:**
- Each dimension: A* (5 pts, above B2) / A (5 pts) / B (3 pts) / C (1 pt) / D (0 pts)
- Applied per part (25 pts each) with weighting across dimensions
- Total: 75 pts

## 2. Sprachbausteine Deep-Dive

### Grammar Points Tested in B2 Teil 1 (ranked by frequency in telc B2 exams)

1. **Passiv with modals** -- Das Problem muss geloest werden. Die Kosten koennen reduziert werden. The most reliable B2 grammar marker.
2. **Konjunktiv II -- advanced forms** -- Beyond wuerde+Infinitiv: haette, waere, but also less common forms like kaeme, gaebe, wuesste. Also Konjunktiv II of modals: haette...koennen, haette...muessen (past unreality).
3. **Konjunktiv I (indirect speech)** -- Er sagte, er sei muede. Die Studie zeige, dass... Appears in journalistic texts in Sprachbausteine. Candidates must recognize the Konjunktiv I forms (sei, habe, koenne) and distinguish them from indicative.
4. **Partizip I and II as attributive adjectives** -- die steigenden Kosten, der gut ausgebildete Mitarbeiter, ein von Experten empfohlenes Verfahren. Extended pre-noun attributes are a hallmark of B2 written German.
5. **Nominalisierung** -- die Erhoehung (from erhoehen), die Bearbeitung (from bearbeiten), die Zufriedenheit (from zufrieden). Nominal style is tested because it dominates formal German prose.
6. **Advanced connectors** -- je...desto, sowohl...als auch, weder...noch, nicht nur...sondern auch, zwar...aber, zumal, insofern als, sofern, es sei denn. These require precise word order knowledge.
7. **Prepositions + Genitiv** -- aufgrund, infolge, angesichts, hinsichtlich, bezueglich, anlaeasslich. These are B2-specific; B1 tests only wegen, trotz, waehrend.
8. **Fixed verb+preposition combinations** -- sich beziehen auf (+Akk), beitragen zu (+Dat), verfuegen ueber (+Akk), abhaengen von (+Dat), beruhen auf (+Dat). More abstract than B1's warten auf, sich freuen ueber.
9. **Passiv-Ersatzformen** -- sich lassen + Infinitiv (Das laesst sich einrichten), sein + zu + Infinitiv (Das ist zu beruecksichtigen), -bar adjectives (umsetzbar, erreichbar). These are the passive alternatives that dominate formal writing.
10. **Relative clauses with prepositions** -- Der Kurs, an dem ich teilgenommen habe... Das Thema, ueber das wir gesprochen haben... Preposition placement before the relative pronoun is a consistent B2 testing point.

### Distractor Patterns in B2 Teil 1

- **Konjunktiv I vs II swap**: habe/haette, sei/waere -- both plausible in reported speech, but only one is correct per context.
- **Passiv vs. active with similar meaning**: "wird...geloest" vs. "loest" -- both could fill a gap, but only the passive fits the formal text register.
- **Connector logic**: dennoch vs. ausserdem vs. infolgedessen -- all are discourse markers but encode different logical relations (concession/addition/consequence).
- **Nominalized vs. verbal form**: "die Reduzierung" vs. "reduziert" vs. "zu reduzieren" -- all related to the same concept but grammatically different.

### Teil 2 Strategy Notes

- B2 word bank texts are often semi-formal reports or informational texts, not personal letters.
- Discourse markers fill ~25-30% of gaps: dennoch, hingegen, demnach, insofern, zudem, ferner.
- Funktionsverbgefuege (function verb constructions) fill another ~20%: in Betracht ziehen, zur Verfuegung stellen, in Anspruch nehmen, in Kauf nehmen, zum Ausdruck bringen.
- The remaining gaps test precise vocabulary: synonyms that differentiate register (fordern vs. verlangen vs. auffordern) and collocations (eine Massnahme ergreifen, einen Beitrag leisten, eine Rolle spielen).
- Unlike B1 where standard letter phrases give "free" answers, B2 requires genuine text comprehension to select from the word bank.

### New at B2 (beyond B1 mastery)

| Topic | How it appears in telc B2 | Testing method |
|-------|---------------------------|----------------|
| Konjunktiv I (indirect speech) | Er sagte, er sei... / Die Studie zeige... | Sprachbausteine gap-fill, Lesen comprehension of journalistic text |

### B1 Grammar That Must Be Solid

These are prerequisite grammar topics not re-taught at B2 but assumed mastered:
- Perfekt with haben/sein (which auxiliary?)
- All cases (Nom/Akk/Dat/Gen) and their article declensions
- Two-way prepositions (Wechselpraepositionen)
- Subordinate clauses with weil, dass, wenn, ob, als, nachdem, bevor (verb-final)
- Relative clauses (standard: der/die/das + case)
- Basic Konjunktiv II (wuerde, haette, waere, koennte)
- Passive voice Praesens (wird + Partizip II)
- Comparative and superlative
- Infinitiv + zu constructions
- Connectors: deshalb, trotzdem, ausserdem, obwohl, damit, um...zu

## 5. What Makes B2 Harder Than B1

This section is critical for content authors to calibrate difficulty correctly.

### Text Length and Density

| Dimension | B1 | B2 |
|-----------|----|----|
| Lesen Teil 2 text length | 350-450 words | 450-600 words |
| Lesen total reading load | ~1,400-1,500 words | ~1,800-2,000 words |
| Sprachbausteine text length | ~150-200 words each | ~200-250 words each |
| Schreiben word count | ~150 words | ~150-200 words |
| Hoeren playback | Teil 1 once, Teil 2+3 twice | ALL parts played ONCE |

### Cognitive Processing Shifts

1. **Implicature in Hoeren**: B1 listening tests whether candidates catch stated facts. B2 tests whether they can distinguish between what a speaker states vs. implies vs. attributes to someone else. An interviewee who says "Manche Leute glauben, dass..." is NOT endorsing that view -- B2 candidates must catch this.

2. **Abstract vocabulary**: B1 texts discuss concrete situations (booking a hotel, visiting a doctor). B2 texts discuss abstract concepts (the impact of digitalization on democratic participation, the tension between economic growth and environmental sustainability).

3. **Argumentation in Sprechen**: B1 speaking requires describing and opining. B2 speaking requires arguing -- presenting a thesis, providing evidence, anticipating counter-arguments, and synthesizing.

4. **Register consistency in Schreiben**: B1 writing accepts informal slips in an otherwise semi-formal letter. B2 writing penalizes register breaks. A Beschwerdebrief that uses "du" instead of "Sie" or drops formal phrases shows register failure.

5. **Nominal style in Lesen/Sprachbausteine**: B2 formal texts use nominal constructions (die Durchfuehrung der Massnahme) rather than verbal ones (dass die Massnahme durchgefuehrt wird). Candidates who cannot decode nominal style will struggle with reading comprehension and fail to produce it in Sprachbausteine.

6. **No second chance in listening**: This cannot be overstated. At B1, candidates hear the long texts twice. At B2, everything is played once. This means candidates must develop note-taking strategies and cannot rely on confirmation listening.

## 7. Schreiben Task Specification

### Prompt Format (for mock authoring)

A B2 Schreiben task consists of:

1. **Two prompt options** (candidate chooses one)
2. Each prompt contains:
   - **Situation** (3-4 sentences): Sets context. Example: "Sie haben vor zwei Wochen im Internet einen Laptop bestellt. Das Geraet ist defekt angekommen. Trotz mehrfacher Kontaktversuche hat der Kundenservice nicht reagiert."
   - **3 Leitpunkte** + optional "eigener Aspekt": Specific content the candidate must address. Example:
     - Beschreiben Sie das Problem genau.
     - Was erwarten Sie als Loesung?
     - Setzen Sie eine Frist fuer die Antwort.
   - **Format instruction**: "Schreiben Sie eine Beschwerde (~200 Woerter). Beruecksichtigen Sie alle Leitpunkte."

### What Makes a Good B2 Schreiben Prompt

- Situation triggers a formal response requiring argumentation or structured complaint
- Leitpunkte require different communicative functions: describing, demanding, justifying, proposing
- Register is unambiguously formal (Sie-form, institutional addressee)
- All 3 Leitpunkte are independently addressable
- The two prompts should offer different text types (e.g., one complaint + one information request)
- Word count target of ~200 words allows developing each Leitpunkt with reasoning

## 8. Sprechen Tasks Specification

### Teil 1: Ueber Erfahrungen sprechen (~5 min total)

Candidates choose from 7 fixed topics. Task card reads something like:
"Waehlen Sie ein Thema aus. Berichten Sie Ihrem Partner / Ihrer Partnerin kurz darueber."

Fixed topics (always the same 7):
1. Ein Buch, das Sie gelesen haben
2. Einen Film, den Sie gesehen haben
3. Eine Reise, die Sie gemacht haben
4. Ein Musikereignis (Konzert o.Ae.), an dem Sie teilgenommen haben
5. Ein Sportereignis, das Sie erlebt haben
6. Eine Person, die fuer Sie wichtig ist
7. Ein Erlebnis, das fuer Sie wichtig war

Sequence: ~1.5-2 min presentation, then partner asks 2-3 follow-up questions.

### Teil 2: Diskussion (~5 min)

Candidates receive a short text (~100-150 words) presenting a controversial position. Both candidates read the same text. Sequence:
1. Express your opinion on the text's thesis
2. Respond to partner's opinion
3. Exchange arguments and examples
4. Attempt synthesis or respectful disagreement

Sample topics: "Sollte Homeoffice zur Pflicht werden?", "Brauchen wir ein Tempolimit auf Autobahnen?", "Soziale Medien: Fluch oder Segen?"

### Teil 3: Gemeinsam etwas planen (~5 min)

A shared planning task with professional-level complexity. Task card includes 5-6 planning dimensions:
- Was genau wollen wir organisieren?
- Wann und wo?
- Wer soll eingeladen werden?
- Was brauchen wir?
- Wie teilen wir die Aufgaben auf?
- Was koennte es kosten?

Sample: "Ihre Abteilung moechte einen Betriebsausflug organisieren. Planen Sie diesen gemeinsam."

### Preparation Time

20 minutes before the oral exam, same as B1. Candidates receive all 3 task sheets and can take notes on paper. No dictionary or electronic aids allowed.

Suggested prep time allocation:
- 5 min: Teil 1 -- choose topic, note key narrative points
- 10 min: Teil 2 -- read text, note pro/contra arguments, prepare Redemittel
- 5 min: Teil 3 -- note planning dimensions, prepare proposals

### Calibration Rules

1. **Vocabulary ceiling**: No word outside B2 range in reading texts or listening scripts. Use the Goethe/Profile Deutsch B2 scope. An occasional C1 word is acceptable ONLY if context makes its meaning transparent.
2. **Grammar ceiling**: Texts may contain C1 structures passively (extended attributes, complex Partizipialkonstruktionen) since candidates only need to comprehend them. Active production (Sprachbausteine, Schreiben) stays strictly within B2 grammar scope.
3. **Distractor quality**: In Sprachbausteine Teil 1, all 4 options must be grammatically plausible words/forms. At least 2 distractors should be tempting because they fit partially (right word class, wrong case/tense/collocation).
4. **No topic overlap within a mock**: If Lesen Teil 2 covers "Digitalisierung am Arbeitsplatz", Hoeren Teil 2 MUST NOT also cover workplace digitalization. Spread across the 16 topic domains.
5. **Text-type authenticity**: Lesen Teil 1 texts should read like real newspaper snippets. Lesen Teil 3 texts should read like real seminar catalogs or job postings. Schreiben prompts should feel like situations candidates might actually encounter.
6. **Listening register**: Teil 1 = broadcast/formal. Teil 2 = interview/conversational-formal. Teil 3 = announcements/presentations. Speakers should use natural hedging (na ja, also, eigentlich) and self-correction in Teil 2.
7. **The "x" option**: In Lesen Teil 3, at least 1-2 of the 10 situations should genuinely have no matching text, requiring candidates to mark "x". Do not make the "x" situations obviously unmatchable -- they should be close to one text but critically different in one detail.
8. **Sprachbausteine completeness check**: After filling all gaps correctly, the completed text must read as natural, fluent German prose appropriate to its register. If the completed text sounds stilted, the item construction is flawed.
9. **Writing sample answers**: Each mock's sample answer must demonstrate all 3 Leitpunkte addressed with B2-level depth (~2-3 sentences per point), consistent formal register, and correct grammar. Sample answer should score B/A on all three criteria.
10. **Speaking prompts diversity**: Across 10 mocks, Teil 2 discussion topics should cover at least 8 of the 16 domain categories. Teil 3 planning scenarios should alternate between professional (Betriebsausflug, Teambuilding) and semi-professional (Sprachkurs organisieren, Nachbarschaftsfest) contexts.

## 12. Quality Markers for Strong B2 Mocks

### What makes a question STRONG

1. **Questions test inference, not scanning.** A B2 reading question should never be answerable by finding a keyword. The correct answer must require synthesizing information from multiple sentences or interpreting implicit meaning.

2. **Distractors use text vocabulary but misapply it.** In MCQ, wrong answers should contain words that appear in the text but combine them into a claim the text does not make. This tests comprehension, not vocabulary.

3. **Sprachbausteine test grammar decisions in context.** A gap where all 4 options are plausible without context, but only one works given the surrounding clause structure -- that is a strong item.

4. **Listening items test the "hedging trap."** When a speaker says "Man koennte argumentieren, dass..." the candidate must recognize this as distancing, not endorsement. Strong items exploit this distinction.

5. **Writing prompts force formal register and argumentation.** The situation must clearly require Sie-form and structured reasoning. Vague prompts that could be answered with a casual email are too easy.

### What makes a question WEAK

1. **Answer is obvious from general knowledge.** If candidates can answer without reading/listening, the question tests nothing.
2. **Only one MCQ option is grammatically possible.** If three options are syntactically impossible in any context, the question is trivial.
3. **Listening script uses unnaturally slow or clear speech.** At B2, audio must use natural tempo with connected speech features (Verschleifungen, reduced vowels, natural intonation).
4. **Sprachbausteine gap has two genuinely correct answers.** Every gap must have exactly one defensible answer. Pre-test rigorously.
5. **Reading texts feel like they were written for the exam.** Authentic register means texts should feel like they were excerpted from a real publication, not generated to contain specific answer words.
